## Abstract

This TIP proposes the implementation of the "Sandwich Defense Loop" within the Aegis Shield protocol to proactively protect users from sandwich attacks on decentralized exchanges (DEXs). The defense mechanism combines predictive algorithms with automated countermeasures to enhance user trading security and mitigate the impact of malicious trading practices.

## Motivation

The prevalence of sandwich attacks undermines user trust and introduces hidden costs in DEX trading. Addressing this issue is crucial to improve the overall user experience, ensuring a safer environment and attracting more users to the Talos ecosystem.

## Specification

The Sandwich Defense Loop will continuously monitor the mempool for large pending orders and detect potential sandwich attack patterns. It will execute countermeasures such as adjusting slippage tolerance, submitting trades to private mempools, and bundling transactions to obscure user intents.

## Rationale

By integrating proactive measures into the trading protocol, the Sandwich Defense Loop exemplifies the Talos vision of leveraging AI to enhance user security autonomously. This feature not only strengthens the ecosystem's protective measures but also aligns with the goal of fostering a user-centric trading environment.

## Security Considerations

- **False Positives:** The mechanism must carefully differentiate between legitimate trades and sandwich attack patterns to minimize disruptions for users.
- **MEV Complexity:** Users may encounter increased complexity and potential fees when utilizing private mempool strategies, necessitating clear user guidance and transparency.
- **Evolving Attacks:** Ongoing adaptation of the RiskAgent's detection algorithms is essential to counteract new strategies employed by attackers seeking to bypass the defense.

## Implementation

The implementation of the Sandwich Defense Loop will involve updates to the existing RiskAgent, the integration of the Delphi Oracle for real-time data analysis, and the development of mechanisms to interact with private mempools and enable transaction bundling.
