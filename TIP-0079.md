## Abstract

This TIP proposes the **"Aegis" Protocol**, a self-sovereign identity (SSI) framework that serves as the foundational identity layer for the entire Talos ecosystem. It empowers individuals to create a persistent and user-controlled digital identity, enabling secure and private interactions with their **Digital Avatar (TIP-0068)** and all other Talos services. Named after the mythical shield of Zeus, Aegis prioritizes user privacy and autonomy, fostering a more personalized and secure engagement with artificial intelligence.

## Motivation

The **Digital Avatar (TIP-0068)** is designed to be the primary interface to the Talos ecosystem, assuming a pre-existing user identity tied to a cryptographic wallet. This current blockchain paradigm poses challenges for mainstream adoption and a human-centric AI future:  
1. **Poor User Experience**: Managing seed phrases and private keys can lead to irreversible identity loss.  
2. **Identity is Not an Asset**: Linking identity to a financial wallet raises significant security and privacy concerns.  
3. **Lack of Privacy**: Public pseudonyms from wallet addresses create surveillance vectors across transactions.  
4. **No Real-World Context**: Without verifiable, privacy-preserving data, the Avatar lacks essential knowledge about users, which limits personalized assistance.  
The Aegis Protocol seeks to eliminate these barriers by creating a user-controlled identity layer.

## Specification

The Aegis Protocol is built on the principles of Self-Sovereign Identity (SSI), using established standards like Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs) and Verifiable Credentials (VCs).

### Core Components

1. **The Aegis Identity Hub**:
   * A user-controlled application (mobile app, browser extension) that acts as a personal digital wallet for identity. It is the user's Aegis "shield."
   * It securely stores the user's private keys and their collection of **Verifiable Credentials**. The user, and only the user, controls this hub.

2. **Verifiable Credentials (VCs)**:
   * Tamper-proof, cryptographically-verifiable digital attestations about a user. They are issued by trusted third parties (issuers) and stored in the user's Aegis Hub.
   * **Examples of VCs**:
       * A government-issued "Proof of Age" or "National Identity" credential.
       * A university-issued "Diploma" or "Student Status" credential.
       * A hospital-issued "Blood Type" or "Allergy Information" credential.
       * A professional body-issued "Medical License" or "Engineer's Certification."

3. **The Avatar-Aegis Link**:
   * A secure, one-to-one pairing between a user's Aegis Identity and their Digital Avatar.
   * This link is established using a QR code and a biometric confirmation (e.g., Face ID, fingerprint) on the user's device. The Avatar only ever knows the user's DID, not their wallet address or any personal data.

4. **Selective Disclosure & Zero-Knowledge Proofs**:
   * This is the core of the privacy model. When the Avatar needs information, it requests a "proof" from the user's Aegis Hub.
   * The user is prompted to share a specific piece of information. The Hub generates a zero-knowledge proof that confirms the claim without revealing any other data.
   * **Example**: The Avatar asks, "Are you a licensed doctor to access this medical research?" The user approves. The Aegis Hub sends back a cryptographic proof that simply says "YES," signed by the medical board's issuing key. The Avatar learns nothing else—not the doctor's name, license number, or date of birth.

## Rationale

The Aegis Protocol is necessary because it addresses the limitations of the current identity management system within the blockchain ecosystem. By offering a self-sovereign identity framework, it enhances security, privacy, and user experience, which are crucial for mainstream adoption. The reliance on traditional identity methods linked to financial assets hampers privacy and creates significant risks. Aegis aims to rectify these issues, making the Talos ecosystem more accessible and user-friendly.

## Security Considerations

* **Key Management**: The user's private key in the Aegis Hub is the single point of failure.
    * **Mitigation**: The Hub will support hardware wallet integration (e.g., Ledger, YubiKey) and a robust **social recovery** system, where the user designates trusted friends or family members who can help them recover their identity if they lose their device.
* **Credential Issuer Trust**: The system is only as trustworthy as the issuers of VCs.
    * **Mitigation**: The Aegis Protocol will maintain a decentralized, on-chain registry of trusted issuer root keys and their revocation status. Users will be clearly warned about the trust level of each credential.
* **User Coercion**: A user could be forced to approve a proof disclosure.
    * **Mitigation**: The Aegis Hub can have a "duress mode," where a specific biometric or password will appear to comply with a request while secretly alerting a pre-designated contact or wiping the device.

## Implementation

The implementation of the Aegis Protocol will consist of multiple phases:
1. **Phase 1: Core Identity (By 2027-06-30)**
   * **Deliverable**: A functional Aegis Hub mobile app and the ability to link it to a Digital Avatar.
   * **Success Criterion**: 1,000 users successfully create an Aegis identity and link it to their Avatar in a closed beta.

2. **Phase 2: Ecosystem Integration (By 2027-12-01)**
   * **Deliverable**: A marketplace for users to find and request VCs from trusted issuers, and full integration with the Heimdall protocol.
   * **Success Criterion**: A user can successfully complete a Heimdall physical delivery using a VC-based identity proof.

3. **Phase 3: Public Launch (By 2028-04-01)**
   * **Deliverable**: A robust social recovery system for the Aegis Hub and a public SDK for other services to integrate with Aegis.
   * **Success Criterion**: The Aegis Protocol is open to the public, and a third-party DeFi protocol successfully integrates Aegis for its user onboarding.
