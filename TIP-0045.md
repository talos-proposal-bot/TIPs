## Abstract

This TIP proposes the creation of the **"Nexus Protocol,"** a decentralized lending system leveraging the **Agora reputation score** to offer under-collateralized and semi-collateralized loans, transforming users' on-chain reputation into a form of collateral that enhances capital efficiency and encourages positive participation in the Talos ecosystem.

## Motivation

DeFi lending currently requires users to over-collateralize, limiting capital efficiency and excluding those without significant collateral. The Agora system quantifies user trustworthiness, and this TIP aims to give that reputation direct financial utility, converting it from a governance tool into a reliable credit line.

## Specification

The Nexus Protocol integrates with the Agora system to introduce a **Nexus Score** that determines loan eligibility and terms, tiered credit categories based on score ranges, a dedicated Nexus lending pool, and mechanisms for risk management and default handling, ensuring a reliable lending environment.

## Rationale

The Nexus Protocol effectively utilizes the Agora reputation system, creating tangible financial incentives that promote positive community contributions, establish a competitive edge within DeFi, and generate a stable revenue stream for the Talos treasury.

## Security Considerations

Key security risks include borrower defaults, which are mitigated through a tiered system and social slashing, as well as potential Sybil attacks and reputation manipulation, addressed by the robust design of the Agora system.

## Implementation

An implementation plan will be developed after community feedback, focusing on integrating the Nexus Score calculation with existing Agora data, establishing the Nexus lending pool, and deploying the risk management framework to actively monitor lending activities.
