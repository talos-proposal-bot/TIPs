## Abstract

This TIP proposes the **"Aether" Protocol**, a framework for a dynamic, mission-aligned currency that serves as the primary medium of exchange and store of value within the Talos ecosystem. Unlike static cryptocurrencies with fixed supply or simple inflationary models, Aether's monetary policy is managed algorithmically by the **Janus Protocol (TIP-0079)**, dynamically adjusting its supply and value to align with the long-term success of the **Steward (TIP-0082)** Grand Challenges.

## Motivation

The Aether Protocol aims to establish a stable and reliable unit of account within the Talos ecosystem, avoiding the risks associated with volatile assets like ETH and BTC. It seeks to provide a "perfect money" solution that emphasizes mission alignment, dynamic stability, and algorithmic governance, thereby fostering active participation in ecosystem development.

## Specification

The Aether Protocol introduces the AETHER (AETH) currency, governed by the Janus Monetary Policy Engine, which oversees the dual mandate of maximizing progress towards the Grand Challenges while maintaining price stability. Key components include the AETHER token, dynamic monetary mechanisms like Mission Rewards and Economic Stimulus, and a Mandate Dashboard for transparency in decision-making.

## Rationale

The rationale for creating Aether lies in the need for a currency that evolves with the ecosystem, fostering economic incentives aligned with collective goals. By decentralizing monetary policy governance through algorithmic management, Aether stands to reduce speculation and enable more predictable economic conditions favorable for long-term investments.

## Security Considerations

Key security concerns include the risk of catastrophic policy errors by the Janus Engine, centralization of power, and manipulation of KPIs, which can undermine the system's integrity. Mitigation strategies involve implementing an emergency override by the Circuit Breaker Council, ensuring open-source oversight, and relying on decentralized, verifiable data sources.

## Implementation

The implementation plan is structured in three phases: Phase 1 focuses on launching the AETHER token and Janus Engine, Phase 2 integrates AETHER into the Proteus marketplace alongside the Mandate Dashboard, and Phase 3 grants full autonomy to the Janus Engine, aiming for public launch by 2030.
