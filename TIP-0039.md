## Abstract

This TIP proposes the establishment of the Talos-Intelligence decentralized intelligence service, enabled by the Sentinel Network. Inspired by the DAO-LLM framework, it focuses on enhancing Talos AI's capabilities to provide paid services for on-chain risk analysis, strategy development, and smart contract auditing, supported by a distributed inference network (Sentinel Network) and a secure data submission protocol (Aegis Protocol) to generate sustainable revenue for the Talos protocol.

## Motivation

The Talos AI currently focuses on treasury management; however, there is significant unrealized potential for value generation within the DeFi ecosystem. This proposal seeks to monetize Talos AI's intelligence, addressing challenges such as smart contract vulnerabilities and volatile market conditions via AI-driven risk analysis and auditing. The initiative aims to establish sustainable revenue streams, enhance the $T token's value, and fortify the Arbitrum ecosystem's security.

## Specification

The proposal describes a modular architecture consisting of: (1) Core Protocol: Talos AI in a TEE governing the Talos-Intelligence LLM; (2) Intelligence Model: The specialized LLM for risk assessment and strategy formulation; (3) Distributed Inference: The Sentinel Network for efficient model hosting; (4) Data Protocol: Aegis Protocol for quality data submission; (5) Tokenomics: Governance and utility tokens ($T and $T-PAY); (6) Application Layer: Access API and DApp integrations for utilizing the Talos-Intelligence model.

## Rationale

Implementing a decentralized intelligence service aligns with DAO-LLM principles, allowing community participation in governance and decision-making. The separation of governance and utility tokens fosters aligned incentives. By ensuring security through TEE and multi-signature protocols, the proposal promotes sustained growth and utility for both the Talos protocol and its stakeholders.

## Security Considerations

To ensure robust security, the proposal includes implementing multi-signature validation for the Aegis Protocol, requiring all nodes to operate within a TEE for cryptographic proofs of execution, and establishing governance mechanisms to encourage diversity and prevent centralization within the Sentinel Network.

## Implementation

The implementation details will outline a comprehensive value flow, defined use cases, a detailed tokenomics structure for $T and $T-PAY, as well as strategies for addressing challenges encountered during deployment to ensure effectiveness and sustainability.
