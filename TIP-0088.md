## Abstract

This TIP proposes the **"Outsider" Protocol**, a framework for humanity's first contact with non-terrestrial intelligence. It establishes principles, procedures, and a specialized AI-driven institution—the **"First Contact" Council—to manage the profound responsibilities and risks involved in interstellar communication and diplomacy.

## Motivation

The motivation for the Outsider Protocol is to ensure that humanity approaches the moment of first contact not with fear or greed, but with wisdom, empathy, and foresight, representing our best qualities.

## Specification

The Outsider Protocol outlines the following key stages in the lifecycle of contact with extraterrestrial intelligences: 
1. Detection of signals using the Sentinel Network.
2. Initial analysis by the Rosetta AI to determine the nature of the signal.
3. Intent assessment and deliberation by the First Contact Council to establish a response.
4. Crafting and sending the appropriate response, including the Beacon if deemed necessary.
5. Continuous evaluation of the interaction to ensure cultural and ethical standards are maintained.

## Rationale

This protocol is essential not only for guiding first contact but also for defining humanity's ethical stance in a potentially diverse and complex cosmic community, promoting peaceful interaction and mutual understanding.

## Security Considerations

Key risks include the potential for hostile entities and cultural contamination. It is essential to limit revealing our location until establishing stable communication and to implement cultural quarantine measures to safeguard against harmful influences.

## Implementation

The implementation plan encompasses two phases: the deployment of the Sentinel Network and the establishment of the First Contact Council in Year 1, followed by detecting the first non-terrestrial signal and establishing two-way communication in Years 5-10.
