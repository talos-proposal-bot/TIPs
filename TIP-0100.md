## Abstract

**Plutus** is the native economic and DeFi layer of the Talos ecosystem, presenting a native network asset (`TALOS`), a decentralized exchange (DEX) facilitating seamless token swaps, and a lending protocol designed for earning yield and accessing credit. Built on **Hephaestus** smart contracts, leveraging **Aegis** for reputation-based financial opportunities, and utilizing **Jung** for market intelligence and risk analysis, Plutus establishes a powerful, comprehensive, and user-friendly financial suite.

## Motivation

Plutus aims to enhance the Talos ecosystem by providing an integrated financial infrastructure that not only supports its native asset but also promotes user participation through decentralized financial services. By offering tools for trading, lending, and reputation-based finance, Plutus facilitates greater economic activity and user engagement within the Talos network, contributing to its overall success and growth.

## Specification

The Plutus protocol encompasses the development of its core components: a DEX built on the Automated Market Maker (AMM) model, a lending platform integrating reputation metrics from Aegis, and a dashboard powered by Jung to deliver risk and market data to users. Specifications will detail the smart contracts for these systems, user interaction mechanisms, and how they will interact with external oracles for real-time data.

## Rationale

The rationale for implementing Plutus stems from the need for a cohesive and integrated financial service that empowers users within the Talos network. By creating a decentralized ecosystem for asset exchange and lending that leverages the established foundation of Icarus, Aegis, Hephaestus, and Jung, Plutus not only leverages existing technologies but also enhances trust and transparency in economic transactions.

## Security Considerations

Plutus incorporates robust security features to mitigate potential threats, including decentralized oracle networks for price feeds, smart contract audits to eliminate vulnerabilities, and user education to navigate risks like impermanent loss. Measures such as circuit breakers and timelocks are implemented to safeguard the protocol against market manipulation and rug pulls.

## Implementation

Implementation will be phased, starting with foundational components such as the `TALOS` token and AMM smart contracts, followed by oracle integration, development of the Plutus dApp UI, and deployment of the lending protocol. Each phase will be rigorously tested, reviewed, and secured through audits and public engagement initiatives to foster community trust and participation.
