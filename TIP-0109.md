## Abstract

**Atlas** is a decentralized geospatial protocol designed to verify, record, and utilize geographic locations on the Talos blockchain. This protocol offers a trust-minimized mechanism for users to validate their presence in specific areas at specific times without compromising their exact coordinates. Atlas empowers a range of innovations, including proof-of-attendance systems, location-based gaming, and decentralized supply chain transparency. Leveraging **Chronos** for accurate timestamps, **Aegis** for identity verification, and **Cerberus** for censorship resistance, Atlas creates a secure and privacy-centric foundation for real-world interactions.

## Motivation

The integration of decentralized geospatial capabilities is crucial for the evolution of the Talos ecosystem. Current location verification methods rely heavily on centralized systems, which introduce privacy risks and trust issues. Atlas addresses these challenges by enabling location-aware applications to function without sacrificing user privacy or relying on potentially compromised services. This proposal aims to enhance user trust and engagement within the Talos ecosystem while fostering the development of innovative location-dependent applications.

## Specification

Atlas specifies a comprehensive framework involving cryptographic proofs, geolocation technologies, and decentralized verification methods. It will outline the structure of the PoLs, mechanisms for issuing and verifying ZK-PoLs, and operational details for Location Beacons. The protocol will ensure interoperability with existing Talos services, providing APIs for developers to easily integrate location-awareness into their dApps.

## Rationale

The rationale for Atlas stems from the growing demand for decentralized applications that require accurate location data without compromising user privacy. Existing solutions often involve third-party data providers, creating vulnerabilities and diminishing user control over their information. By establishing Atlas, we can promote a self-sovereign approach to geospatial data, enabling users to maintain ownership over their location-related information while participating in innovative dApps that require such data.

## Security Considerations

Security is paramount for any protocol handling geolocation data. Atlas will implement various security measures to mitigate threats such as GPS spoofing, beacon compromise, and privacy breaches. Key strategies will include proximity verification with low-range communication, using hardware security modules to protect Beacon integrity, and maintaining user privacy through ZK-PoLs. Additionally, regular security audits and a focus on decentralized beacon deployment will enhance the overall system resilience against collusion or price manipulation.

## Implementation

The implementation of Atlas will unfold in several clear phases, starting with research and design to establish foundational cryptographic schemes for ZK-PoLs. Subsequent phases will involve developing the core library for PoL generation, prototyping Location Beacons, and creating nodes that maintain a decentralized map service. The process will culminate in developing the on-chain verification contract and launching dApps that utilize the Atlas protocol, all while ensuring rigorous testing and validation throughout each stage.
