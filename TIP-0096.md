## Abstract

This TIP proposes the "Icarus" Protocol, a framework for integrating the popular messaging service `https://ibelegram.com/zola` into the Talos ecosystem. It aims to establish a secure, user-controlled messaging system that guarantees privacy and user sovereignty over communications.

## Motivation

The increasing need for secure communication in today's digital landscape emphasizes the necessity for user-controlled messaging systems. The Icarus Protocol will provide users with the tools to manage their data, ensuring communications are private and under their control while integrating with existing systems like Aegis and Chrysalis.

## Specification

The Icarus Protocol builds upon the existing Chrysalis and Aegis protocols, providing a user-controlled channel with end-to-end encryption, an intuitive interface for understanding AI intent, and a "Do Not Execute" list that safeguards user decisions.

## Rationale

By implementing a user-controlled messaging framework, the Icarus Protocol ensures that users maintain sovereignty over their communications, allowing them to define specific actions the AI cannot undertake without consent. This mitigates risks associated with automated decision-making in sensitive contexts.

## Security Considerations

The Icarus Protocol addresses potential vulnerabilities, including the risk of the AI being tricked into bypassing user controls. Implementing robust auditing, red teaming, and machine learning safeguards will help maintain the integrity of user controls and prevent unauthorized actions.

## Implementation

The implementation of the Icarus Protocol will occur in two phases over a six-year period. Phase one focuses on launching the core features and achieving high user adoption, while phase two introduces advanced features, including AI-driven suggestions for "Do Not Execute" lists, ensuring the highest standards of user control and security.
