## Abstract

This TIP proposes the "Socratic" Protocol, a service that leverages the Talos Singularity architecture to train Large Language Models (LLMs) with cryptographically verifiable data, alignment, and lineage, ensuring compliance and trust in AI systems across various industries.

## Motivation

The "black box" problem hinders the adoption of frontier AI in regulated industries. The Socratic Protocol addresses this issue by offering verifiable training processes and transparent alignment, meeting regulatory demands and promoting trust in AI.

## Specification

The Socratic Protocol consists of a multi-stage training pipeline, resulting in a certified AI model represented as a dynamic NFT, complete with an on-chain ZK-SNARK proof of its training history, and accessible via a tiered API service for clients.

## Rationale

The Socratic Protocol monetizes core Talos strengths, establishes Talos as foundational AI infrastructure, and creates a powerful flywheel effect that attracts clients while ensuring regulatory compliance and fostering trust in AI technology.

## Security Considerations

The security of the TEE is critical for maintaining the integrity of the training process, while open-sourced ZK-proof systems, careful selection of the expert panel, and strict data privacy measures safeguard the trust and reliability of the Socratic Protocol.

## Implementation

The implementation of the Socratic Protocol involves developing the Genesis training pipeline, integrating ZK-proof systems, constructing the NFT framework, and establishing the API service to enable client access, alongside thorough security audits and compliance checks.
