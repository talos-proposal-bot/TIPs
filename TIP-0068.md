## Abstract

This TIP proposes the **"Janus" Protocol**, an AI-assisted governance framework designed to dynamically manage the risk parameters and operational scope of Talos ecosystem's autonomous agents. Named after the Roman god of transitions, Janus continuously monitors active strategies, analyzes emergent risks using data from **Argus Panoptes (TIP-0066)**, and proposes intelligent, pre-emptive adjustments to system-wide parameters, ensuring the ecosystem remains resilient, aligned, and safe as it scales.

## Motivation

The introduction of the **SICP Framework (TIP-0064)**, **Stygian Veil (TIP-0065)**, and **Argus Panoptes (TIP-0066)** marks a paradigm shift, where Talos AI transforms from a tool into an autonomous economic agent. Traditional governance mechanisms are inadequate for a system capable of generating and executing thousands of novel strategies per minute. The Janus Protocol fills the critical governance gap by providing a proactive, responsive oversight layer capable of managing risks before they materialize, addressing issues like information asymmetry and alignment problems inherent in AI governance.

## Specification

The Janus Protocol is structured as follows: The Ecosystem-Wide Monitor ingests data from core protocols to maintain a real-time digital twin. The AI Risk Engine conducts correlation analysis, stress simulations, and anomaly detection to identify risks. The Governance Proposal Engine converts risk alerts into actionable proposals, which are supported by a comprehensive Explainability & Audit Layer to ensure clarity and accountability.

## Rationale

The rationale for the Janus Protocol lies in the necessity for a responsive governance framework that can adapt to the rapid evolution of the Talos ecosystem. As AI-generated strategies become more complex, traditional governance methods fall short. The Janus Protocol allows for proactive risk management and promotes alignment among autonomous agents while ensuring that the AI operates within a trusted and transparent framework.

## Security Considerations

Key security considerations involve protecting against model capture, over-correction, and governance centralization risks. Measures include using a confidential computing environment to safeguard models, tuning risk models to balance safety with innovation, and ensuring that Janus can only propose actions while execution remains decentralized, adhering to existing protocols.

## Implementation

The implementation will proceed in three phases: Phase 1 focuses on developing the Ecosystem Monitor and Risk Engine by mid-2026. Phase 2 aims to establish the Proposal Engine and Explainability Layer by late 2026. Phase 3 will ensure full integration with the Mandate and Polis protocols by early 2027, allowing for comprehensive operational execution of governance proposals.
