## Abstract

This proposal outlines the Sandwich Defense Loop (SDL), a protective layer within the Aegis Shield Protocol aimed at preventing sandwich attacks on decentralized exchanges (DEXs). It details the mechanisms, customizable user-defined risk profiles, and integration with existing systems to enable proactive transaction protection.

## Motivation

The increasing prevalence of sandwich attacks in decentralized trading environments poses a significant risk to users' transactions and capital. The Sandwich Defense Loop (SDL) addresses this challenge by providing a structured approach to detecting and mitigating such risks before execution, enhancing user confidence and security in DEX operations.

## Specification

The SDL operates within the Aegis Shield’s Transaction Guard stage, executing before trade broadcasts. It includes features such as dynamic slippage control, gas price randomization, and customizable risk profiles for user-specific parameters. The implementation leverages existing simulation engines to evaluate potential attacks and guide user decisions.

## Rationale

By incorporating the SDL into the Aegis Shield Protocol, users gain the ability to adapt their trading strategies based on their risk tolerance. This not only protects individual users but also contributes to the integrity and stability of DEX ecosystems by reducing the incidence of successful sandwich attacks.

## Security Considerations

The SDL must ensure compliance with user-defined permissions to prevent unauthorized actions. It will employ capability tokens to manage access and verify permissions before executing any adjustments or transaction broadcasts, enhancing the overall security of transaction processing.

## Implementation

The implementation of the SDL involves several key steps: integrating the SDL module into the Aegis Shield guard pipeline, enhancing the user interface for risk profile management, ensuring compliance with permission settings for various actions, and conducting thorough unit and integration tests to validate the system's robustness.
