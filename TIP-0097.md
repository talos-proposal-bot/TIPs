## Abstract

Aegis is a decentralized identity and reputation protocol enabling Talos users to demonstrate trustworthiness without central authority. It utilizes self-sovereign Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs) for identity verification, integrates with Icarus (TIP‑0093) for attestations, and leverages a tamper-proof distributed ledger for reputation management.

## Motivation

The motivation behind Aegis is to empower users with control over their own identities while fostering a trustworthy ecosystem. By eliminating reliance on central authorities, Aegis aims to enhance privacy and security, allowing users to build and prove their reputations in a decentralized environment.

## Specification

Aegis will leverage DIDs for self-sovereign identity, utilizes zero-knowledge proofs for privacy-preserving attestations, and employs a distributed ledger for immutable and verifiable reputation storage.

## Rationale

The rationale for implementing Aegis lies in the need for a decentralized solution to identity and reputation that enhances user autonomy and mitigates risks associated with centralized control such as data breaches and identity theft.

## Security Considerations

Potential threats to the Aegis system include Sybil attacks, privacy leakage, and ledger spam. Mitigations include starting new identities with zero reputation, using encryption for sensitive data, and implementing rate-limiting mechanisms for transaction submissions.

## Implementation

The implementation will be phased, starting with Planning & Design, followed by the Core Identity Library development, Reputation Ledger deployment, ZKP Module creation, and culminate in a public rollout that includes security audits and community engagement for feedback.
