## Abstract

Echo is a decentralized protocol designed to empower users by enabling them to create, manage, and monetize their own social graphs. Unlike traditional social media platforms that centralize control, Echo gives users ownership of their identity, relationships, and content. The protocol outlines standards for social interactions, profile management, content feeds, and community engagement, leveraging integrated technologies like Aegis for identity, Icarus for messaging, Calliope for content, and Plutus for monetization. The innovative Social Graph NFT (SG-NFT) encapsulates a user's complete social presence, allowing seamless migration across Echo-compatible applications.

## Motivation

The motivation behind the Echo Protocol is to redefine how social interactions occur in the digital landscape, shifting power from centralized platforms to individual users. By fostering a system where users maintain ownership of their social graphs and identities, Echo addresses issues of privacy, platform lock-in, and censorship. It aims to cultivate a more open, transparent, and user-centric internet ecosystem that empowers individuals and promotes innovation in social application development.

## Specification

The Echo Protocol specifies the construction and interactions of the Social Graph NFT (SG-NFT), profile contracts, connection actions, and content feed contracts. It defines metadata standards for user profiles, the mechanics of connection actions (like follow and friend requests), and detailed algorithms for content aggregation in feeds. The specification will also outline integration processes with existing protocols such as Aegis, Icarus, Calliope, and Plutus to ensure a cohesive user experience across all functionalities.

## Rationale

The rationale for developing the Echo Protocol is rooted in the need for a paradigm shift in how social media operates today. Current platforms often suppress user autonomy, leading to issues like censorship, data exploitation, and lack of transparency. Echo tackles these issues by allowing users to retain complete control over their digital identities and social networks. This approach not only enhances user agency but also incentivizes positive community interactions and furthers decentralized innovations in the ecosystem.

## Security Considerations

Security considerations for the Echo Protocol include the prevention of Sybil attacks through the implementation of Aegis reputation scores, mitigating graph spam by introducing small fees for connection actions, and ensuring the confidentiality of private communications through end-to-end encryption in Icarus. Additionally, the protocol employs selective disclosure of verifiable credentials to protect users' real-world identities and facilitates on-chain verification of algorithmic behavior to safeguard against malicious manipulations.

## Implementation

The implementation of the Echo Protocol will occur in multiple phases, starting with research and design to finalize standards for SG-NFTs and social graphs. Subsequent phases will entail developing the core protocol library, deploying essential smart contracts, and creating the first frontend application that utilizes the Echo Protocol. Critical milestones will include a comprehensive security audit and strategies for ecosystem growth to support third-party developers in building compatible applications.
