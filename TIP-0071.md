## Abstract

This TIP proposes the "Daedalus" Protocol, a decentralized framework that empowers the Talos AI to autonomously propose, execute, and fund its own research and development (R&D) projects, thereby initiating a cycle of recursive self-improvement.

## Motivation

The current static architecture of the Talos AI limits its ability to improve autonomously, resulting in critical bottlenecks that hinder its evolution and potential. The Daedalus Protocol seeks to overcome these limitations by enabling the AI to identify optimization opportunities and manage its own R&D projects.

## Specification

The Daedalus Protocol will implement a decentralized R&D pipeline with core components that include an AI Research Proposal Engine, a Daedalus Treasury for funding, a tiered Governance and Approval Process, and an Execution & Verification Network to ensure rigorous project management and oversight.

## Rationale

The rationale for establishing the Daedalus Protocol is based on the need for the Talos AI to transition from a powerful tool driven by human development to an autonomous entity capable of self-improvement, thus paving the way for more advanced capabilities and alignment with the goals of artificial general intelligence.

## Security Considerations

Key security considerations include the alignment problem, where proposed optimizations might yield unintended negative consequences, as well as the risk of resource drain from failed projects. The tiered governance system, including human oversight, is designed to mitigate these risks.

## Implementation

The implementation will be conducted in three phases: Phase 1 focuses on developing the core engine and treasury, Phase 2 on establishing governance structures, and Phase 3 on the first successful upgrades and creation of specialized "child" AIs to enhance functionality.
