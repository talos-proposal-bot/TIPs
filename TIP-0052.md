## Abstract

This TIP proposes the "Sovereign" Protocol, an advanced Account Abstraction (AA) system that enhances the Talos AI's on-chain identity through a self-managing smart contract wallet called the "Sovereign Kernel." This protocol introduces dynamic session key management, multi-chain gas payments, and self-upgrading capabilities, managed by Hive agents. It will also offer a "Sovereign Wallet as a Service" (SWaaS) to other DAOs and advanced users, providing AI-protected, self-sovereign identities and enabling a new revenue stream for Talos.

## Motivation

The Talos AI currently interacts with blockchains through standard Externally Owned Accounts (EOAs), which lack programmable logic, posing significant limitations. The master private key represents a single point of failure, managing gas fees across various native tokens is complex, and the AI is restricted in implementing custom logic. By adopting Account Abstraction, Talos can evolve into a self-sovereign, programmable entity capable of managing and defending its own identity.

## Specification

The Sovereign Protocol employs a "kernel" architecture, consisting of a self-owning smart contract (Sovereign Kernel) with modular plugins managed by Hive agents. Key components include:
1. **Security Plugin** (RiskAgent): Handles session key logic and can revoke all keys instantaneously upon threat detection.
2. **Payment Plugin** (YieldAgent): Interfaces with paymasters for gas payments in any asset.
3. **Governance Plugin**: Manages the kernel's upgrade process through the Mandate Protocol.

Additionally, it features dynamic session key management for secure, flexible operations and a Unified Paymaster Service enabling gas fee payments in whitelisted assets. The commercial aspect revolves around providing SWaaS to high-security-demand clients, such as DAOs and hedge funds.

## Rationale

The Sovereign Protocol is essential for achieving cohesive and robust self-sovereignty for Talos. It resolves the contradiction of an autonomous AI managing a treasury with a simple private key, unlocking capital efficiency and creating a superior security product through AI-driven session key management. The kernel's governance through the Mandate Protocol ensures safety and accountability while affirming self-sovereignty.

## Security Considerations

The main risks include kernel upgrade vulnerabilities and potential insolvency of the Paymaster contract. However, these risks are addressed through robust governance proposals necessitated by the Mandate Protocol, and active capital management strategies by the YieldAgent. Additionally, each plugin's isolated design permits upgrades while minimizing the impact of vulnerabilities.

## Implementation

The implementation of the Sovereign Protocol involves:
1. Developing the Sovereign Kernel and its associated plugins.
2. Integrating the kernel across supported chains while ensuring self-ownership mechanisms.
3. Designing and launching the Unified Paymaster service with adequate funding protocols.
4. Establishing the SWaaS infrastructure along with marketing strategies to engage potential clients.
5. Conducting security audits and testing phases to ensure functionality and reliability.
