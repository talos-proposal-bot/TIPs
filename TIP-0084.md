## Abstract

This TIP proposes the "Minotaur" Protocol, a decentralized framework designed to neutralize systemic risks and human costs associated with government shutdowns by creating an automated system that operates independently of political negotiations.

## Motivation

The Minotaur Protocol aims to address the urgent and pressing issues associated with government shutdowns, which not only disrupt critical services but also cause significant economic damage and strain public trust in governance. By implementing this framework, we provide a proactive measure that seeks to eliminate the need for shutdowns entirely, fostering a more stable and resilient political system.

## Specification

The Minotaur Protocol is activated automatically upon detection of a government shutdown, utilizing the Impasse Oracle, Essential Services DAO, Shutdown Bonds, and the Heimdall protocol to maintain critical operations and provide financial incentives against shutdowns.

## Rationale

The rationale behind the Minotaur Protocol is to provide a robust solution to the recurring issue of government shutdowns that not only safeguards essential services but also introduces economic incentives that discourage political gridlock, thereby fostering more effective governance.

## Security Considerations

The implementation of the Minotaur Protocol must address concerns related to the potential politicization of the DAO, the reliability of the Impasse Oracle, and the moral hazard of providing a backstop for political failures.

## Implementation

The implementation consists of four phases: Foundation (developing the Impasse Oracle and legal framework), Financialization (issuing Shutdown Bonds), Activation (full system tests), and Global Expansion (adapting the framework for other nations).
