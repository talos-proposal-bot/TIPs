## Abstract

This TIP proposes the **"SICP Framework"**, a metalinguistic abstraction layer for the Talos AI, enabling dynamic design, interpretation, and execution of novel financial protocols and smart contracts in response to emergent market conditions or user-defined goals, transforming Talos from a reactive system into a proactive, generative intelligence.

## Motivation

The need for adaptable, innovative, and user-friendly DeFi protocols within the Talos ecosystem highlights the limitations of static, hard-coded systems. The SICP Framework addresses these issues by allowing Talos to create its own protocols, thereby increasing adaptability, fostering innovation, and enhancing user expression.

## Specification

The SICP Framework introduces a multi-layered architecture for generative protocol creation, consisting of components such as the Abstract Syntax Tree (AST) Core, Talos Strategy Language (TSL), Interpreter & Formal Verifier, and Generative AI Engine, each designed to enhance the AI's ability to dynamically generate and manage financial protocols.

## Rationale

The SICP principles emphasize that powerful systems emerge from creating expressive languages rather than accumulating features. By adopting this approach, the SICP Framework empowers the Talos AI to independently innovate and address complex financial problem-solving through custom-built protocols.

## Security Considerations

The implementation of the SICP Framework must address potential risks such as model poisoning, unbounded complexity, and ensuring oracle integrity. This involves strict resource limits for the interpreter, careful curation of training data, and the incorporation of robust multi-source data validation mechanisms.

## Implementation

The implementation of the SICP Framework will be executed in three phases: Phase 1 (Foundation) focuses on finalizing the AST and TSL specs; Phase 2 (Intelligence) aims to integrate the Generative AI engine; and Phase 3 (Integration & Governance) integrates governance protocols and prepares for mainnet deployment.
