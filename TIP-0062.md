## Abstract

This TIP proposes a new feature for the **Aegis Shield** protocol, introducing a **"Sandwich Defense Loop"** within the `RiskAgent`. This feature proactively defends users against "sandwich attacks," a predatory trading practice that disrupts transactions on decentralized exchanges (DEXs). The system detects potential attacks and provides users with a tiered set of countermeasures to choose their preferred level of protection.

## Motivation

Sandwich attacks exploit the public nature of blockchain transactions, leading to hidden costs for users of DEXs. This TIP aims to empower users by giving them control over their trading experience, promoting a more secure and efficient trading environment while minimizing user frustration.

## Specification

The proposed "Sandwich Defense Loop" will include features such as:
- A confidence threshold customizable by the user that must be met before the defense mechanism is triggered.
- Clear user prompts to confirm defensive actions and the option to execute trades despite AI recommendations.
- User-defined risk profiles to tailor protection levels based on their individual trading strategies.
- Guidance elements that explain the potential costs and liquidity implications of using private mempool strategies.

## Rationale

Implementing the "Sandwich Defense Loop" is essential to mitigate the negative effects of sandwich attacks on users. By allowing traders to select their desired level of defense, we ensure that the protective measures align with individual trading goals, ultimately enhancing user satisfaction and trust in the Talos ecosystem.

## Security Considerations

Considerations include:
- Minimizing false positives to ensure legitimate trades are not disrupted.
- Providing users with clear explanations of actions taken by the AI and allowing for manual overrides.
- Maintaining strict limits on agent autonomy through user-defined permissions.
- Implementing continuous learning mechanisms while ensuring that feedback is aligned with community values.

## Implementation

The implementation will involve updating core agents to integrate insights gained from previous protocols (Cognition and Autonomy). This includes:
- Developing the `RiskAgent` to incorporate an `EmpathyEngine` for analyzing both market data and sentiment.
- Introducing new methods for proactive threat assessment and user interaction.
- Ensuring the `SovereignProtocol` verifies agent actions and maintains compliance with user-defined permissions.
