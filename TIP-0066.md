## Abstract

This TIP proposes the **"Stygian Veil" Protocol**, a confidential computation layer that enables the Talos AI to process sensitive data and execute private strategies without revealing the underlying inputs, logic, or intermediate states to the public or to untrusted nodes. It achieves this by integrating Secure Multi-Party Computation (MPC) and, in the long term, Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) into the Talos core, creating a "black box" environment for AI operations.

## Motivation

The **SICP Framework (TIP-0064)** empowers the Talos AI to generate novel, sophisticated financial strategies. However, on-chain transparency is a double-edged sword. When a strategy's logic, data sources, and execution triggers are public, it creates severe risks such as strategy front-running, intellectual property theft, data privacy violations, and adversarial attacks. Without a layer of confidentiality, advanced AI capabilities will be limited to non-sensitive tasks, affecting real-world utility. The Stygian Veil aims to address this fundamental constraint.

## Specification

The Stygian Veil is an opt-in, cryptographic layer that sits between the user's request and the Talos AI's execution environment, supporting two modes of confidential computation, phased over time: 
1. **Secure Multi-Party Computation (MPC)**: Splits computation among decentralized nodes to compute functions without revealing plaintext.
2. **Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE)**: Allows computation directly on encrypted data.

## Rationale

The rationale behind the Stygian Veil is to enhance the privacy and security of sensitive computations while maintaining the innovative capabilities of the Talos AI. By implementing MPC and FHE, the protocol aims to protect user data, output privacy, and computation processes, enabling high-value and sensitive tasks while mitigating risks associated with on-chain transparency.

## Security Considerations

Key management and trust models are critical to the Stygian Veil's security. Secure key generation and distribution for MPC/FHE, along with decentralized key ceremonies and hardware integration, are essential. The economic model must incentivize honest behavior among nodes, and protection against data poisoning and adversarial attacks is necessary to maintain integrity.

## Implementation

The implementation will be divided into three phases: 
1. **Phase 1 (By 2026-03-30)**: Establish a network of functional Veil Nodes running a rigid MPC library. 
2. **Phase 2 (By 2026-09-01)**: Conduct research and develop a prototype for FHE feasibility and performance benchmarks. 
3. **Phase 3 (By 2027-01-01)**: Complete integration with the SICP Framework and validate through a successful, private mainnet transaction.
